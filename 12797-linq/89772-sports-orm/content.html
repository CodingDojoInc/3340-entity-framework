Now in our HomeController, let's look at our example query:

    ViewBag.BaseballLeagues = context.Leagues
                .Where(l => l.Sport.Contains("Baseball"));

ViewBag you should be familiar with, and you should have seen references to the queries .Where and .Contains and how to create these queries on the page before. But what about context and Leagues? Well, look up! "Leagues" matches the Leagues we see in Context.cs. And context is what we've called our connection to the database. 

The code reads like this: 

In ViewBag.BaseballLeagues, query into my database (context) and look into my table for Leagues. From my Leagues table, I want all the leagues Where the Sport (a property of the League model) Contains the word "Baseball". Now in our ViewBag, we have a list of all the Leagues where the sport is baseball. On our cshtml side we can render this information in a table, and voila! Our information is rendered!

Don't worry if this seems like a lot of confusing information. We'll be breaking this down in detail in the next section. In general your queries will look like this:

ViewBag.<Name of bag> = context.<Name of table>.<Your query goes here>
